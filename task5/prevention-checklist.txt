Stay Safe Online - Checklist for College Students in India


1.	Use strong, unique passwords
Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across multiple apps. 


2.	Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security. 


3.	Be cautious with unknown links and attachments
Don’t click on suspicious links in emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS—especially those promising prizes or urgent actions. 


4.	Verify profiles before interacting
Fake accounts are common. Check mutual friends, activity history, and profile authenticity before engaging. 


5.	Limit personal information sharing
Avoid posting sensitive details like your phone number, address, college ID, or daily routine publicly. 


6.	Use secure Wi-Fi networks
Avoid logging into banking or personal accounts on public Wi-Fi. Use a VPN if necessary. 


7.	UPI Safety Tip #1: Never share OTP or UPI PIN
No bank or payment app will ask for your PIN or OTP. Sharing it can lead to instant financial loss. 


8.	UPI Safety Tip #2: Double-check payment requests
While using apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm, confirm whether you are sending or receiving money before approving any request. 


9.	Log out from shared devices
Always log out of your accounts when using cyber cafés or friends’ devices. 


10.	Keep your devices updated
Regularly update your phone and apps to fix security vulnerabilities. 


11.	Report suspicious activity immediately
If you face cyber fraud or bullying, report it at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930 for quick assistance. 
Staying alert and practicing these habits can significantly reduce your risk online.
